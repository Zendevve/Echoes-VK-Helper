## Why source-available instead of open source

The source is intentionally under PolyForm Noncommercial rather than
AGPL/MIT/Apache so that:

- The maintainer can fund continued development by selling prebuilt
  binaries and commercial licences.
- Forks can read everything, learn from it, submit PRs, and build the
  wizard for their own non-commercial use.
- Forks **cannot** build the EXE and give it away for free, undercutting
  the paid binary — that would be commercial use, which the
  noncommercial licence prohibits.
- A commercial licence is a one-time, per-version transaction: no
  royalties, no SaaS-style per-seat fees, no surprises.
